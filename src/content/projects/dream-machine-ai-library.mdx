# Dream Machine A.I. × Library Project

Public libraries are the most democratic classrooms in America — free to enter, trusted by their neighborhoods, and open to everyone. When Dream Machine Innovation Lab set out to bring generative AI education to underserved communities — with Google, Chime, and Operation HOPE behind it — the library was the obvious venue. What the tour needed was a curriculum that could meet a total beginner on a Tuesday afternoon and leave them building by evening.

## From fear to fluency

Most people's first feeling about AI isn't curiosity — it's fear. Fear of being replaced, fear of being left behind, fear of looking foolish in front of a tool everyone else seems to understand. So the workshops are built to move people from fear to fluency: break the concepts down in plain English, put the tools in their hands the same hour, and ground all of it in emotional intelligence and digital wellness — so participants leave not just knowing how to use AI, but how to use it well.

Whether someone walks in as an aspiring technologist, an entrepreneur, a creator, or simply curious, they leave having built something real with the latest tools — and connected to a community that's moving forward with them.

## The four tracks

Marlon authored the core curriculum around one principle: **no prior tech knowledge required — just curiosity.** Four tracks, each moving from live demonstration to hands-on practice:

**01 — Advanced prompting for business growth.** Entrepreneurs and professionals use generative AI on their actual work: grant proposals, research reports, business plans, marketing strategies. Advanced prompting techniques for precise, creative outputs — with examples tailored to small-business needs.

**02 — Marketing with AI.** How to run impactful campaigns in the digital age: generating marketing copy, social posts, and ad campaigns; AI-driven audience targeting and trend analysis; building a consistent brand voice with AI assistance.

**03 — AI and creativity.** AI as a creative instrument — hands-on practice generating images, music, and video, techniques for folding AI into artistic and professional projects, and an honest look at how these tools are reshaping creative industries.

**04 — The future of work.** What AI means for your career path: the growing demand for AI skills, where the opportunities are opening across industries, and how to develop AI literacy as a competitive advantage.

The curriculum isn't the only thing Marlon built. Every slide deck, live demo, and hands-on lab across the four tracks is his work — designed so a total beginner could follow along and be building in the same hour.

## Using it well is part of the curriculum

The hard part was never teaching the tools — today's AI tools are genuinely easy to start with. The hard part was the second question every room asks once the fear breaks: *"Should I trust this?"* That's why digital wellness and emotional intelligence are woven into every track, not bolted on at the end. The sessions deliberately trade coverage for confidence: fewer tools, more reps, and time to practice with support in the room — because a participant who leaves with one workflow they actually trust beats one who leaves with ten they'll never open again.

## The tour

What started as a five-city pilot became a national movement: **eleven cities and counting**, from Brooklyn to San Francisco, Detroit to Memphis. **More than 2,000 people** have come through the workshops so far — students, seniors, entrepreneurs, and career-changers, many touching generative AI for the first time. Every stop hosted in a public library.

## Why it matters

Access compounds — and so does its absence. The A.I. × Library Project is equity-by-design in practice: if the next wave of technology is going to reshape work, the on-ramp has to run through every neighborhood, not just the ones with conference badges.
